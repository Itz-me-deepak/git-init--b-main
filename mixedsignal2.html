Data Converter Design (Mixed-Signal Focus)

Course Title: Data Converter Design (Mixed-Signal Focus)

Introduction

This course focuses on the design and analysis of high-performance ADCs and DACs, which are the heart of mixed-signal systems. It combines both analog precision and digital calibration techniques to develop robust data conversion architectures.

Importance

High-resolution and high-speed data converters are essential in applications like imaging, communication, audio processing, and instrumentation. Mastery of these designs enables innovation in any mixed-signal product.

Why Choose This Course?

  • Dive into Flash, SAR, Sigma-Delta, Pipelined, and Nyquist-rate ADC/DAC designs.
  • Analyze performance metrics like SNR, SFDR, ENOB, and power efficiency.
  • Learn calibration, clocking, and digital correction methods.
  • Ideal for aspiring mixed-signal SoC designers and data converter architects.

Course Outcomes & Advantages

  • Model and simulate various converter architectures.
  • Understand analog-digital interface intricacies.
  • Implement calibration and error correction strategies.
  • Build design skills for industries like telecom, medical electronics, and consumer devices.

Duration

6 to 8 weeks

Mode

Online / Offline

Includes

Simulation tasks, circuit modeling, and performance evaluation.
