HDL Basics (Verilog or VHDL)

Course Title: HDL Basics (Verilog or VHDL)

Introduction

This course introduces Hardware Description Languages (HDLs) — essential tools for digital system design. You’ll learn how to model digital circuits using Verilog or VHDL, which are used to describe, simulate, and synthesize logic for FPGAs and ASICs.

Importance

HDLs are the backbone of digital hardware design. Proficiency in Verilog or VHDL is a must for FPGA developers, digital designers, and verification engineers.

Why Choose This Course?

  • Master syntax, semantics, and constructs of Verilog or VHDL.
  • Learn to describe combinational and sequential logic.
  • Practice testbench writing and functional simulation.
  • Build reusable modules and understand behavioral vs structural modeling.

Outcomes & Advantages

  • Write clean, synthesizable code for digital circuits.
  • Understand simulation and debugging techniques.
  • Gain readiness for advanced RTL design and FPGA implementation.
  • Ideal for anyone entering the ASIC, FPGA, or embedded design domains.

Duration

4 to 6 weeks

Mode

Online / Offline

Includes

Coding exercises, simulations using tools like ModelSim/Xilinx Vivado, and mini-projects.
