Analog Circuit Design

Course Title: Analog Circuit Design

Introduction

Analog Circuit Design is the cornerstone of electronics engineering, focusing on the design and analysis of circuits that process continuous signals. From basic amplifiers to complex analog systems, this course covers the foundational principles that drive communication systems, audio devices, sensors, and power management technologies.

Importance of the Course

Analog circuits are crucial in bridging the real world and digital systems. Despite the digital revolution, analog design remains a vital skill due to its presence in signal processing, instrumentation, RF communication, and mixed-signal systems. Mastery of analog design ensures a deeper understanding of how hardware truly works.

Why Choose This Course?

  • Learn to design, simulate, and test real-world analog systems.
  • Gain practical skills with tools like SPICE simulation and hands-on circuit building.
  • Develop critical thinking in noise analysis, stability, and signal integrity.
  • Taught by experienced professionals from VLSI and embedded domains.
  • Foundation for careers in core electronics, semiconductor, and IoT sectors.

Course Outcomes and Advantages

  • Design and analyze linear and nonlinear analog circuits.
  • Understand and apply concepts of biasing, feedback, and frequency response.
  • Simulate circuits using EDA tools and interpret performance parameters.
  • Prepare for roles such as Analog Design Engineer, PCB Designer, and Mixed-Signal Verification Engineer.
  • Build a strong foundation for advanced studies in VLSI and embedded systems.
