Data Converter Design

Course Title: Data Converter Design (Analog Focus)

Introduction

This course focuses on the design of Analog-to-Digital Converters (ADC) and Digital-to-Analog Converters (DAC)—essential components that bridge analog real-world signals with digital processing systems.

Importance of the Course

Data converters are found in every modern electronic system: from audio and video devices to medical instrumentation and sensor interfaces. A strong understanding of converter architecture and analog precision is vital for high-performance applications.

Why Choose This Course?

  • Learn both system-level and transistor-level design of ADCs and DACs.
  • Explore various architectures like Flash, SAR, Sigma-Delta, and R-2R.
  • Strong emphasis on analog precision, noise, linearity, and power optimization.
  • Highly relevant for careers in mixed-signal IC design and SoC development.

Course Outcomes and Advantages

  • Understand the working of different data converter topologies.
  • Analyze key performance metrics like ENOB, INL/DNL, SNR, and THD.
  • Design, simulate, and optimize data converters using tools like Cadence or LTspice.
  • Gain a solid foundation for analog/mixed-signal IC design roles.

Course Duration

6 to 8 weeks

Mode

Online / Offline

Includes

Theory sessions, simulation-based design labs, and a final mini-project.
