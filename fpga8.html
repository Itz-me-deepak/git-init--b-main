FPGA-based SoC Design

Course Title: FPGA-based System-on-Chip (SoC) Design

Introduction

This course teaches the design and integration of full SoCs using soft-core or hard-core processors on FPGAs. You'll explore how CPUs, memory, peripherals, and custom logic blocks come together in one chip.

Importance

FPGA-based SoCs are key to embedded systems, edge computing, and robotics. Knowing how to integrate processors and custom logic expands your design capabilities exponentially.

Why Choose This Course?

  • Work with processors like MicroBlaze (Xilinx), Nios II (Intel), or RISC-V cores.
  • Understand memory mapping, bus interfaces (AXI, AHB), and peripheral integration.
  • Combine HDL, C programming, and embedded development workflows.
  • Build full systems with hardware/software co-design.

Outcomes & Advantages

  • Build your own embedded processor-based systems.
  • Develop hybrid applications with custom IPs and software logic.
  • Interface FPGAs with Linux/RTOS if desired.
  • Excellent preparation for embedded SoC development roles.

Duration

6 to 8 weeks

Mode

Online / Offline

Includes

SoC design labs, custom IP development, and real-time project integration.
