Mixed-Signal Circuit Design

Course Title: Mixed-Signal Circuit Design

Introduction

Mixed-signal circuit design bridges the gap between analog and digital worlds. This course covers the principles and techniques needed to design integrated circuits that handle both continuous (analog) and discrete (digital) signals in a unified system.

Importance

Mixed-signal ICs are essential in applications like data converters, audio systems, sensor interfaces, and wireless transceivers. With the increasing demand for smart devices and IoT products, skilled mixed-signal designers are more valuable than ever.

Why Choose This Course?

  • Understand design challenges at the analog-digital boundary.
  • Explore key building blocks: comparators, PLLs, ADCs/DACs, and clock recovery circuits.
  • Learn about layout considerations, noise issues, and mixed-signal simulation.
  • Build a strong foundation for careers in SoC, automotive electronics, and communication ICs.

Course Outcomes & Advantages

  • Design and simulate core mixed-signal circuits.
  • Understand isolation, grounding, and noise mitigation techniques.
  • Gain exposure to Cadence Virtuoso, Spectre, or other EDA tools.
  • Suitable for analog/mixed-signal IC design roles.

Duration

Mode
