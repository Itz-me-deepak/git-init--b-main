Course Title: Introduction to FPGA Architecture

Introduction

Field-Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGAs) are reconfigurable hardware devices used in a wide range of applications — from digital signal processing to embedded systems and high-speed data processing. This course introduces the internal architecture, logic resources, and applications of FPGAs.

Importance

FPGAs offer flexibility, parallelism, and speed unmatched by general-purpose processors. Understanding their structure and behavior is essential for modern embedded and hardware engineers.

Why Choose This Course?

  • Learn about logic blocks, routing resources, DSP slices, and memory elements inside FPGAs.
  • Explore the differences between FPGAs, ASICs, and microcontrollers.
  • Understand how real-time applications are mapped to hardware.
  • Start your journey into hardware acceleration, reconfigurable computing, and system prototyping.

Outcomes & Advantages

  • Understand the internal structure and functioning of FPGAs.
  • Identify suitable applications for FPGA deployment.
  • Lay a strong foundation for learning HDL and FPGA-based design tools.
  • Build confidence to begin RTL implementation and verification.
